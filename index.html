About Me

Hi! I'm Bennett, an Aerospace Engineering B.S. student at UC San Diego from the class of 2021. Recently, I had the opportunity to teach high school students in Rwanda how to build and fly drones - one of my favorite hobbies and passions. In the future, I aim to explore and advance the aerospace industry through meaningful and impactful work.

Current Involvement

Controls Lead Engineer, Team Pilot

UCSD Design Build Fly (DBF)

As one of the few members with experience in remote-controlled (RC) vehicles, I was chosen to be the team pilot and controls lead, and also aided in many other subteams such as electronics, structures, propulsions, and more. This year, our competition plane will have a wingspan >4 ft, carry a payload, and have a rotating radome - I can't wait to start building!

Colossus SFS Engineer

Students for the Exploration and Discovery of Space (SEDS)

Colossus is a static test-fire stand completely built by UCSD students. As a member of the structures and plumbing subteam, I'm responsible for redesigning the calibration method of the load cell. Along with my partner, we designed an improved system utilizing pulleys, springs, and a high-powered winch that will increase accuracy, and are currently in the process of building it.

Drone Class Instructor

World Mission Secondary School (WMSS) in Kigali, Rwanda

This summer, I traveled to Rwanda along with a team of 6 to host a Summer Tech Camp at WMSS. A lot of preparation was done for the trip - I was responsible for creating the entire curriculum, as well as organizing and bringing all the materials needed for the class - but I loved every second of it. I was thoroughly inspired by all my bright students, who surpassed my expectations and were ecstatic to learn about drones and technology.

Past Experience

Science Project - Solar Thermoelectric Generator

August 2015 – June 2016

Cupertino High School

What if you could capture the sun's radiated heat and turn it into something useful? To answer this question, I came up with an innovative idea - a device that utilizes a Thermoelectric Generator (TEG) to convert heat into electricity. It worked, showing a maximum theoretical efficiency limit way higher than solar panels, also earning me several awards at prestigious science fairs.

COSMOS

July 2016 – August 2016

California State Summer School for Mathematics and Science

A distinguished program for STEM students - this is where I learned how to design and build model airplanes, building my fundamental knowledge of Aerospace Engineering. 3D printed parts, XFLR5 analysis, composite fabrication and mathematical calculations were all used in this project.

FRC Team 2473

Sept 2016 - Sept 2017

FIRST Robotics

    As part of the hardware team, I helped CAD, prototype, and test a passive object-holding mechanism - which turned out to be the most vital part of our robot. Due to its simplicity and effectiveness, our robot advanced to our first World Championships in >7 years, where we ended as finalists in our division.

Ongoing Projects
