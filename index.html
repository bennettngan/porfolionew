About Me

Hi! I'm Bennett, an Aerospace Engineering B.S. student at UC San Diego from the class of 2021. Recently, I had the opportunity to teach high school students in Rwanda how to build and fly drones - one of my favorite hobbies and passions. Below, you will see a timeline of my engineering milestones, starting from the beginning.

Past Experiences

Science Project - Solar Thermoelectric Generator

Cupertino High School

What if you could capture the sun's radiated heat and turn it into something useful? To answer this question, I came up with an innovative idea - a device that utilizes a Thermoelectric Generator (TEG) to convert heat into electricity. It worked, showing a maximum theoretical efficiency limit way higher than solar panels, also earning me several awards at prestigious science fairs.

COSMOS

California State Summer School for Mathematics and Science

A distinguished program for STEM students - this is where I learned how to design and build model airplanes, building my fundamental knowledge of Aerospace Engineering. 3D printed parts, XFLR5 analysis, composite fabrication and mathematical calculations were all used in this project.

FRC Team 2473

FIRST Robotics

As part of the hardware team, I helped CAD, prototype, and test a passive object-holding mechanism - which turned out to be the most vital part of our robot. Due to its simplicity and effectiveness, our robot advanced to our first World Championships in >7 years, where we ended as finalists in our division.
